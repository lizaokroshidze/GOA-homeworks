Leonardo da Vinci

Birth: April 15, 1452

Death: May 2, 1519

Leonardo da Vinci was a Italian painter, draftsman, sculptor, architect, and engineer, widely recognized as one of the greatest artists of all time.

Notable Works: His most famous paintings include the Mona Lisa and The Last Supper.

Contributions: Da Vinci was not only an artist but also a scientist and inventor, conceptualizing ideas such as the helicopter, tank, and various anatomical studies.

Philosophy: He believed in the importance of observation and the scientific method, which he applied to both art and science.

Legacy: Leonardo's notebooks, filled with sketches and ideas, reveal a mind that was centuries ahead of its time, embodying the Renaissance humanist ideal.
